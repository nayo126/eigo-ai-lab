## TL;DR
- A viral r/ChatGPT thread asked users which single tweet most changed how they use ChatGPT, surfacing a clear pattern in the replies
- The most-cited prompts fall into five repeatable shapes: critique-first, role assignment, step-by-step reasoning, output format locking, and memory anchoring
- All five can be stored as Custom Instructions or saved prompts in under ten minutes and reused across every future chat

## What happened

A thread on r/ChatGPT titled "Life before this tweet" asked users to share the one tweet or prompt that most changed how they work with ChatGPT. The replies, which spread across X and LinkedIn within hours, did not converge on a single magic phrase. Instead they clustered around five recurring prompt patterns that long-time users say turned ChatGPT from a novelty into a daily tool.

The most-quoted reply pointed to the "critique my work before answering" pattern, where the user pastes a draft and asks ChatGPT to act as a skeptical editor before producing a rewrite. Other top replies referenced the "act as a senior X" role prompt popularised on X in 2023, the "think step by step" reasoning trigger that originated in academic prompting research, structured output prompts that lock JSON or markdown shape, and the use of Custom Instructions and the Memory feature to make these patterns permanent.

What the thread captured, more than any individual prompt, is the shift from one-shot questions to reusable templates. Commenters reported that saving three or four prompts as Custom Instructions cut their average session length and reduced the back-and-forth needed to get usable output.

## Why it matters

For freelancers, indie hackers and small teams paying for ChatGPT Plus or the API, prompt reuse is the cheapest productivity lever available. OpenAI has not raised the price of ChatGPT Plus in 2026, but token costs on GPT-5 and the longer reasoning budgets on o-series models mean that wasted prompts now cost real money on the API side.

The competitive angle is also shifting. Claude Sonnet 4.6 and Gemini 3 both ship with their own equivalents of Custom Instructions and Projects, and Anthropic's Claude.ai now supports saved Skills that behave similarly to the patterns surfaced in the Reddit thread. A user who has internalised the five prompt shapes can move between ChatGPT, Claude and Gemini without rebuilding a workflow from scratch, which weakens the lock-in advantage of any one vendor.

For content creators and SEO operators, the thread is also a reminder that the most durable AI tips are not model-specific. "Act as a senior editor and find three weaknesses before rewriting" works on GPT-5, Claude Opus 4.7 and Gemini 3 with minor tweaks. Vendor-specific tricks date quickly; structural prompts do not.

## Try it yourself

1. Open ChatGPT, go to Settings, Personalization, and Custom Instructions. In the "How would you like ChatGPT to respond" box, paste: "Before answering any request, restate the goal in one sentence, list the top two risks of a naive answer, then respond. Use markdown headings." This bakes the critique-first pattern into every new chat.

2. Save a role prompt as a reusable template. A workable starter: "Act as a senior [role] with ten years of experience. Push back on weak assumptions, ask one clarifying question if the brief is ambiguous, and only then produce the deliverable." Swap [role] for editor, growth marketer or staff engineer depending on the task.

3. For any task that involves logic, math or multi-step planning, append "Think step by step and show your working before the final answer." This is the cheapest reasoning boost available and still measurably improves accuracy on GPT-5 and Sonnet 4.6 according to public benchmarks.

4. Lock output format up front. End drafting prompts with "Return the response as a markdown table with columns: Section, Draft, One-line rationale." Locked formats are easier to scan, easier to paste into Notion or Linear, and reduce the temptation to re-prompt for tidier output.

5. Turn on Memory in ChatGPT and feed it three to five durable facts about the work: target audience, preferred tone, word-count ceilings, and any house style rules. Memory persists across chats, so these no longer need to be re-stated in every session. Equivalent setups exist as Projects in Claude and as Gems in Gemini.

Readers who already use Custom Instructions can audit them once a quarter. Prompts that worked on GPT-4 sometimes underperform on GPT-5 because the newer model already does more reasoning by default; verbose "think step by step" wrappers can be trimmed without losing quality.
